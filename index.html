Research


An Agent-Based Modeling of COVID-19: Validation, Analysis, and Recommendations.


Supervised by: Dr. M. Sohel Rahman (Professor, CSE, BUET)

Published: February 19, 2021
Paper published at: Cognitive Computation
(Special Issue: Data-Driven Artificial Intelligence approaches to Combat COVID-19)

View paper in journal's website

This study introduces an agent-based model simulating COVID-19 transmission in a city, customizable with location-specific parameters. Validated against Ford County, KS data, it evaluates interventions in a scaled-down New York City, showing that contact tracing with over 60% smartphone adoption and lockdowns can reduce effective reproduction number below 1 in 3 weeks. With 75% smartphone use, infections are eliminated within 3 months, and tracing emergency workers in low smartphone areas can help control spread.


Segmentation of Lung Tumor from CT Images using Deep Supervision


Supervised by: Dr. M. Sohel Rahman (Professor, CSE, BUET)

Published: March 07, 2022
Paper published at: Computers in Biology and Medicine

View paper in journal's website

This study uses two-dimensional wavelet transform and neighboring CT slices for lung tumor segmentation, experimenting with U-Net and MultiResUNet. The best performance was achieved with Deeply Supervised MultiResUNet, achieving a dice coefficient of 0.8458.


Predicting transcriptional activation domain function using Graph Neural Networks


Supervised by: Dr. Daisuke Kihara (Professor, CS, Purdue University)

Status: Submission in progress

Analyzing transcriptional activation domains is challenging due to their sequence diversity and disordered nature. This study uses graph neural networks (GNNs) to predict their functionality, outperforming traditional methods with a residue-level GNN that achieved 97.9% accuracy. Key features for predicting functionality include amino acid composition, acidic/aromatic residue abundance, and amphipathic properties.

An Agent-Based Modeling of COVID-19 for Bangladesh


Supervised by: Dr. M. Sohel Rahman (Professor, CSE, BUET)

Status: Submission in progress

This study introduces an Agent-Based Model to simulate COVID-19 spread in Bangladesh, categorizing agents into various health states and age groups. Using a "Contact Matrix," the model examines the impact of non-pharmaceutical interventions on infection trends. It combines agent-based and compartmental modeling, enabling scalable simulations for the entire population of Bangladesh.
